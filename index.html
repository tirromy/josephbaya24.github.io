FACTORS AFFECTING EGG PRODUCTION DEEP LITTER SYSTEM OF POULTRY FARMING IN LIMURU, KIAMBU COUNTY.

Overview Focus is to investigate the factors affecting egg production in deep litter system of poultry farming in limuru, kiambu county.

Objectives To determine the factors that influence egg production. Specific objectives; To determine how mortality influences egg production. To determine how the amount of feeds influences egg production.

Methodology Utilized Multiple Linear Regression Model analysis techniques including ANOVA. Performed data cleaning and feature engineering in R, and created interactive visualizations using Excel

Key Findings The level of significance used was 5%. Mortality had a significant effect on feeds since the p-value was less than 5%. Amount of feeds consumed had no effect on egg production since the p-value was relatively large.

Challenges Consistency of the data. Some data was incomplete and some outdated which could potentially interfere with the data analysis and data accuracy. The data that was available had just a few predictor variables therefore analyzing it was quite challenging. Some of the data analysis results may be incorrect as a result of the minimum number of predictor variables.

Tools and Technologies R, Multiple Linear Regression, ANOVA, Excel

Factors Affecting Access to Credit in Kenya (Mombasa)

Overview: The focus is to determine the factors that determine individual access to credit in Kenya (Mombasa County).

Objectives: To establish factors that enhances and those that limit individual access to credit in Mombasa County. To draw policy implications and make regarding recommendations access to credit in Mombasa County.

Methodology: The determinants of credit availability were examined in this study using logit regression models. This method was selected due to the result variable's discrete dichotomous nature, which is a household's access to credit status. Performed data cleaning and feature engineering in R studio, and created interactive visualizations using Excel to present the data.

Key Findings/Results: The logistic regression model employed, as evidenced by the deviance statistics, provided a reasonable fit for the data, thereby establishing a foundation for comprehending the relationship between the selected determinants and the ability to access credit.Based on the data, we find that Approximately 38.98% of households sought and accessed credit while 61.02% of the total sampled population were found to be credit constrained. However, 86.44% of household sought credit.

Challenges and Solutions: The use of secondary data may have restricted the variables' range, making it more difficult to capture the impacts of determinants of access to credit. Furthermore, the outcomes might have been impacted by biases that existed during the data collection procedure.

Tools and Technologies: R, logistic Rehression, Excel
